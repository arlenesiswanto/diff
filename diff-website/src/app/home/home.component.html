differentially private datasets

we aim to make data accessible


In sharing large datasets with rich user content, data that personally identifies distinct users should not be fully shared due to privacy concerns. The nature of data can also be sensitive: health data, financial information, etc.

However, big data from users is very valuable and useful in data analysis, machine learning, and other applications of artificial intelligence and statistical analysis. Diff allows users to query datasets without compromising client privacy.

why differential privacy matters

Differential privacy is the model that makes it possible to provide accurate statistics from a dataset while maintaining a high level of privacy. Diff adds randomness to datasets such that individual entries are randomized while properties of the dataset that are useful for analysis are preserved.

◉

Health
A researcher at a hospital owns data quantifying the immunological weakness episodes that current patients of an experimental drug are using. She wants to publish this information due to promising results, but does not want to violate the HIPAA law of privacy and security of health information.

◉

Free Speech
A journalist-whistleblower in a region with high censorship and retaliation policies wants to release documented numeric information of inhuman working hours for a certain factory division. A piece of information that identifies one worker will result in their punishment.

◉

Finance
A financial institution wants to publish the median credit scores of customers that participated in its pilot loan program, which had few users, without leaking information that can identify them.

◉

Policy
A public policy analyst wants to share the average height dataset of students at a local school before and after a new meal program was instituted at the school without releasing identifying information for these minors.

how diff works


Most research relies heavily on data analysis. However, consumer privacy and patient confidentiality often prevent owners of data from distributing potentially valuable datasets.

Diff brings differential privacy to publicly available datasets. Differential privacy makes it possible to provide accurate statistics from a dataset while maintaining client privacy.
